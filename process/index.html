Export Process

Indonesian Export Process for Coffee, Spices, Palm Sugar, Rice By-products, and Bamboo Handicrafts

Our export process helps international buyers understand product inquiry, specification discussion, availability confirmation, packing coordination, documentation support, and shipment communication for selected Indonesian products.

Main Export Flow

Eight steps from inquiry to delivery coordination.

01

Inquiry Review

We review product interest, form, quantity, destination, shipment timing, and packing requirements.

02

Specification Alignment

Buyer and seller align quality target, product form, packaging, documentation needs, and commercial terms.

03

Sourcing & Availability

We confirm availability from trusted supply channels and prepare the best matching product option.

04

Preparation & Sorting

Products are selected, checked, sorted, packed, or prepared based on their category, availability, and confirmed buyer requirements.

05

Quality & Packing Check

We review packaging suitability, labeling needs, and shipment handling before export preparation.

06

Documentation Support

Commercial invoice, packing list, and other destination-specific documents are coordinated as required.

07

Freight Coordination

Ocean freight, container planning, or sample air shipment is arranged according to the agreed plan.

08

After-shipment Communication

Shipment updates and follow-up communication support long-term buyer relationships.

Export Documents

Documentation depends on product and destination.

International buyers may need different documents depending on the product category, destination country, and import rules. We coordinate document preparation according to the confirmed transaction.

Common discussion points

  • Commercial invoice and packing list
  • Certificate of origin when required
  • Phytosanitary or fumigation needs where applicable
  • Bill of lading and freight details
  • Product specification and packing declaration
